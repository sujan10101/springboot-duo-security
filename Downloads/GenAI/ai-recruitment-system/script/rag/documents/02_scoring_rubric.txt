ROLE-SPECIFIC SCORING RUBRICS
==============================
Version 3.1 | Maintained by: Talent Acquisition Team | Last Updated: Q1 2025

PURPOSE: These rubrics ensure consistent, fair, and role-relevant evaluation. Reviewers must apply
these criteria to every candidate. A candidate may be accepted with a score of 75+ if strengths
in one area meaningfully compensate for gaps in another. Hard rejections occur below 60.

---

RUBRIC: AI/ML ENGINEER
=======================

CATEGORY 1: Core ML Fundamentals (25 points)
- 25 pts: Demonstrates deep understanding of optimization (gradient descent variants, learning rate scheduling),
          model evaluation (precision/recall/F1, confusion matrices, ROC-AUC), regularization techniques,
          and statistical concepts (bias-variance tradeoff, hypothesis testing). Can explain WHY, not just HOW.
- 20 pts: Solid understanding of most fundamentals with minor gaps in advanced topics.
- 15 pts: Understands standard algorithms (linear regression, trees, SVMs) but limited depth in deep learning theory.
- 10 pts: Basic ML knowledge; can implement tutorials but cannot reason about design choices.
- 0–5 pts: Superficial familiarity; cannot explain concepts under probing.

CATEGORY 2: Deep Learning & Framework Proficiency (25 points)
- 25 pts: Proficient in PyTorch or TensorFlow; built and trained custom architectures (CNNs, RNNs, Transformers).
          Experience with fine-tuning pre-trained models. Understands memory/compute trade-offs.
- 20 pts: Strong framework skills; used pre-built architectures but limited custom model design.
- 15 pts: Can train standard models; debugging skills developing. Limited fine-tuning experience.
- 10 pts: Followed tutorials successfully; cannot troubleshoot independently.
- 0–5 pts: Minimal hands-on experience.

CATEGORY 3: LLM / GenAI Experience (20 points) [NEW — weight increased from v2.0]
- 20 pts: Hands-on with LLM fine-tuning (LoRA/QLoRA), RAG pipelines, prompt engineering with systematic
          evaluation, embedding models, and vector stores. Understanding of alignment and safety considerations.
- 15 pts: Experience with LLM APIs and prompt engineering; may have built RAG demos. Limited fine-tuning.
- 10 pts: Familiar with calling LLM APIs; has used ChatGPT or similar tools for projects.
- 5 pts: Theoretical knowledge only; no hands-on LLM project.
- 0 pts: No LLM experience.

CATEGORY 4: MLOps & Production (15 points)
- 15 pts: Experience with model serving (TorchServe, TensorFlow Serving, FastAPI), monitoring (data drift,
          model degradation), experiment tracking (MLflow, W&B), and containerization. CI/CD for ML.
- 10 pts: Model serving experience and basic experiment tracking. Limited production monitoring.
- 5 pts: Can containerize a model; no real production deployment experience.
- 0 pts: Development only; no deployment experience.

CATEGORY 5: Data Engineering & Preprocessing (15 points)
- 15 pts: Expert in data pipelines (Pandas, Spark, or SQL at scale), feature engineering, handling class
          imbalance, data versioning, and dealing with real-world messy data. Caught leakage in a challenge.
- 10 pts: Solid preprocessing skills; understands and applies standard techniques.
- 5 pts: Can clean standard datasets; limited experience with real-world data messiness.
- 0 pts: Relies on pre-cleaned tutorial data only.

SCORING THRESHOLDS (AI/ML Engineer):
- 90–100: Strong hire; senior-level consideration
- 80–89: Confident hire; mid-level
- 70–79: Hire with minor reservations; junior-mid level; clear growth areas identified
- 60–69: Borderline; hire only with manager sign-off and strong cultural/growth signal
- Below 60: Reject; fundamental gaps that cannot be addressed in onboarding

RUBRIC: BACKEND ENGINEER
=========================

CATEGORY 1: Server-Side Programming (25 points)
- 25 pts: Expert in at least one backend language (Python/Java/Node.js/Go); has built production systems
          at scale. Deep understanding of concurrency, async patterns, and memory management.
- 20 pts: Strong proficiency; production experience with minor gaps in advanced concepts.
- 15 pts: Mid-level; can build standard CRUD APIs but limited experience with complex business logic.
- 10 pts: Beginner-intermediate; primarily tutorial-based projects.
- 0–5 pts: Very limited coding ability; cannot implement beyond basic functions.

CATEGORY 2: Databases & Data Management (25 points)
- 25 pts: Expert SQL (complex joins, window functions, query optimization, indexing strategy); experience
          with NoSQL (MongoDB, Redis, Elasticsearch); schema design for maintainability and performance.
- 20 pts: Strong SQL; basic NoSQL; some query optimization experience.
- 15 pts: Can write correct SQL; limited optimization or schema design experience.
- 10 pts: Basic SQL (CRUD operations only); no NoSQL experience.
- 0–5 pts: Minimal database experience.

CATEGORY 3: System Design & Architecture (25 points)
- 25 pts: Experience designing distributed systems; understands trade-offs (CAP theorem, eventual consistency);
          microservices vs monolith; API design (REST, GraphQL); caching strategies.
- 20 pts: Has designed moderately complex systems; understands microservices; limited distributed experience.
- 15 pts: Monolithic application design; beginning to understand service decomposition.
- 10 pts: Can build a simple REST API; limited architectural thinking.
- 0–5 pts: No system design experience beyond tutorials.

CATEGORY 4: Cloud & DevOps (15 points)
- 15 pts: Hands-on AWS/GCP/Azure (compute, storage, databases, serverless); Docker + Kubernetes in production;
          CI/CD pipelines; infrastructure as code.
- 10 pts: Cloud experience (EC2, RDS, S3); Docker in production; CI/CD basics.
- 5 pts: Has deployed to cloud but limited hands-on; Docker locally only.
- 0 pts: No cloud experience.

CATEGORY 5: Security & Reliability (10 points)
- 10 pts: Understands authentication (JWT, OAuth), authorization patterns, SQL injection prevention,
          rate limiting, circuit breakers, and observability (logging, metrics, tracing).
- 7 pts: Implements auth correctly; limited distributed reliability patterns.
- 3 pts: Basic security awareness; no reliability engineering experience.
- 0 pts: No awareness of security or reliability considerations.

SCORING THRESHOLDS (Backend Engineer):
- 90–100: Strong hire; senior-level consideration
- 80–89: Confident hire; mid-level
- 70–79: Hire with minor reservations; junior-mid level
- 60–69: Borderline; only with manager sign-off
- Below 60: Reject

RUBRIC: FRONTEND ENGINEER
==========================

CATEGORY 1: Core Frontend Technologies (30 points)
- 30 pts: Expert HTML5/CSS3; advanced JavaScript/TypeScript; deep React (hooks, state management, performance
          optimization, reconciliation); responsive and accessible design (WCAG).
- 25 pts: Strong React and TypeScript; solid CSS; some accessibility knowledge.
- 20 pts: Proficient in React; JavaScript (may not use TypeScript); standard CSS.
- 10 pts: Can build simple UI; limited component architecture thinking.
- 0–5 pts: Beginner; tutorial-only projects.

CATEGORY 2: State Management & Architecture (20 points)
- 20 pts: Expert in state management (Redux, Zustand, Jotai, or React Query); understands component
          architecture, code splitting, and performance profiling.
- 15 pts: Redux or equivalent; understands lifting state and Context API; some performance work.
- 10 pts: Context API; limited state management experience beyond useState.
- 5 pts: Minimal state management; single-component apps.
- 0 pts: No experience.

CATEGORY 3: Testing & Quality (20 points)
- 20 pts: Comprehensive testing strategy: unit (Jest), integration (Testing Library), E2E (Cypress/Playwright);
          >80% coverage on recent projects; CI-integrated testing.
- 15 pts: Unit and integration tests; limited E2E; good coverage on critical paths.
- 10 pts: Some unit tests; testing added as afterthought.
- 5 pts: Minimal testing; knows testing concepts but hasn't applied them.
- 0 pts: No testing experience.

CATEGORY 4: Build Tools & Performance (15 points)
- 15 pts: Expert with Webpack/Vite; bundle optimization (code splitting, tree shaking, lazy loading);
          Core Web Vitals; performance profiling tools.
- 10 pts: Familiar with build tools; some bundle optimization.
- 5 pts: Uses create-react-app or similar without custom configuration.
- 0 pts: No build tool experience.

CATEGORY 5: Design Collaboration & UX Sensibility (15 points)
- 15 pts: Strong Figma skills; implements pixel-perfect designs; deep accessibility understanding; collaborates
          effectively with designers; makes proactive UX improvement suggestions.
- 10 pts: Can implement designs accurately; basic Figma; limited accessibility.
- 5 pts: Implements designs with some gaps; limited designer collaboration.
- 0 pts: No design collaboration experience.

SCORING THRESHOLDS (Frontend Engineer):
- 90–100: Strong hire; senior-level consideration
- 80–89: Confident hire; mid-level
- 70–79: Hire with minor reservations; junior-mid level
- 60–69: Borderline; manager sign-off required
- Below 60: Reject

---

GENERAL EVALUATION NOTES (ALL ROLES):
- Transferable skills should be credited (e.g., R → Python, Angular → React) with a small discount
- Side projects count as valid experience if they demonstrate real problem-solving
- Research experience counts, especially if it shows depth and rigorous thinking
- Communication quality during the interview adds up to ±5 points (soft-skill bonus)
- Strong cultural alignment and growth mindset can move a borderline candidate (65–69) to hire
